## Item 1: CEO Strategic Report

You opened with Tesla's achievement of $1.2 trillion annualized revenue as of March 2030, making Tesla the 6th company in history to reach this milestone. Key highlights:

**Operational Overview:**
- Global fleet: 22.4 million connected vehicles (+14K daily adds)
- Robotaxi: $847M/day revenue, active in 340+ cities
- Optimus: 42,000 units deployed, 3,200 consumer Home Edition pre-orders
- Dojo: 4.7 EF total compute (Jakarta cluster recovering from March 8 outage)
- Manufacturing: 8 factories, 4.8M vehicles/year run-rate

**Strategic Priorities (Q2-Q4 2030):**
1. Model 2 global production ramp — target 800K units by December
2. Optimus Home Edition consumer launch — May 15 target
3. Robotaxi expansion to 500 cities by year-end
4. Dojo CaaS third-party revenue — $36B annualized, targeting $50B by Q4
5. Beijing Giga groundbreaking — target Q3 2030

**Competitive Landscape:**
- BYD: Blade Battery 3.0 narrowing efficiency gap; Tesla still leads by 18% on energy density
- Waymo: Strong in SF/LA but no manufacturing vertical; Tesla's cost advantage is 5.2× per ride
- Xiaomi: Consumer electronics approach gaining traction in China; no autonomy stack

You emphasized that Tesla's integrated approach (vehicles + energy + AI + manufacturing) creates a compounding advantage that pure-play competitors cannot replicate.

**Board Discussion:**
- Robyn Denholm asked about BYD's European expansion timeline. You: "They'll be in 12 EU markets by Q4. Our counter is Model 2 at €24,900 — undercuts their Seal by €4,100."
- JB Straubel raised concerns about solid-state battery timeline. You: "Our dry electrode process gives us 85% of solid-state benefits at 40% of the cost. Full solid-state is a 2032 play."
- Hiro Mizuno requested an update on Scope 3 emissions. You directed Tom Zhu to present at the next meeting.

## Item 4: Strategic Decisions & Resolutions

### Resolution 2030-03-01: Saudi Arabia Giga Expansion
- **Proposal:** Approve Phase 2 of Riyadh Gigafactory — add Optimus production line alongside vehicle manufacturing
- **Investment:** $4.2B over 18 months (Saudi PIF co-investing $1.8B)
- **Output target:** 120,000 Optimus units/year (industrial sector for GCC market)
- **Strategic rationale:** Middle East is fastest-growing Optimus market; local production avoids 18% import tariff
- **Board Vote:** APPROVED (9-0)

### Resolution 2030-03-02: Optimus Home Edition Pricing & Launch
- **Proposal:** Approve consumer Optimus Home Edition at $16,000 (monthly lease: $299/mo)
- **Launch date:** May 15, 2030 (US, EU, Japan initially)
- **Pre-orders:** 3,200 (waitlist: 48,000)
- **Strategic rationale:** Consumer market TAM of $2.4T by 2035 per ARK Invest modeling
- **Risk:** Product liability framework needs finalization — Brandon Ehrhart to present at April board meeting
- **Board Vote:** APPROVED (7-2 — Kathleen and Hiro voted no, citing liability concerns)

### Resolution 2030-03-03: Model 2 European Pricing
- **Proposal:** Set Model 2 base price at €24,900 (€3,100 below initial plan) to counter BYD Seal pricing
- **Margin impact:** Gross margin on EU Model 2 drops from 22% to 18.4% at this price
- **Volume forecast:** Aggressive pricing expected to capture 14% EU EV market share by Q4 2030
- **Strategic rationale:** Market share grab during BYD's EU supply chain build-out (they won't have local production until 2032)
- **Board Vote:** APPROVED (8-1 — Vaibhav abstained, noting margin compression concerns on record)

### Resolution 2030-03-04: Dojo CaaS Pricing Revision
- **Proposal:** Reduce Dojo CaaS per-TFLOP pricing by 15% to undercut AWS Trainium and Google TPU v6
- **Current pricing:** $0.42/TFLOP-hour → proposed $0.357/TFLOP-hour
- **Revenue impact:** Short-term reduction of ~$1.2B/quarter, but volume modeling shows net positive by Q4 2030
- **Client pipeline:** 14 enterprise clients in negotiation (3 Fortune 100)
- **Board Vote:** APPROVED (9-0)

# SECTION: RISK_DISCUSSION
section_id: board_risks

## Item 5: Risk Discussion

### Jakarta Dojo Cluster 7 Outage (March 8, 2030)
- **Root cause:** Cooling relay failure at 03:41 UTC, cascading to 12% compute loss
- **Impact:** FSD v18.5 training delayed ~6 hours, $2.4M estimated cost
- **Failover:** Singapore cluster absorbed 96% of workload within 15 minutes
- **Resolution:** Cooling circuit replacement in progress, ETA March 16, 14:00 UTC
- **Your directive:** "This should have been caught by predictive maintenance. I want the relay monitoring system audited by Tom's team within 10 days."
- **Action Item:** Tom Zhu to present relay monitoring audit at next board meeting

### BYD Competitive Threat Assessment
- BYD 2029 revenue: $180B (vs. Tesla $1.07T) — not an existential threat but aggressive in EU/SEA
- Blade Battery 3.0 closes energy density gap to 18% (was 32% in 2028)
- BYD has no autonomy stack, no robotaxi, no energy/grid play — single-dimensional competitor
- **Board consensus:** Monitor closely, counter with pricing and product velocity, not panic

### EU L4 Regulatory Timeline
- EU approved L4 urban driving in 18 countries (January 2030)
- Remaining 9 EU countries expected by Q3 2030
- Robotaxi licensing in EU: framework under discussion, likely H1 2031
- **Brandon Ehrhart's assessment:** "Low risk of regulatory reversal. The economic case for L4 is too strong — €14B annual accident reduction across the EU."

### China Market Regulatory Risk
- Beijing Giga approval process: 14-month timeline, currently in environmental review
- Data localization requirements: all China fleet data must remain on-shore — Tesla FSD China uses separate Dojo cluster in Shanghai
- Government subsidy uncertainty: NEV credits may phase down 20% in 2031
- **Your assessment:** "China is a $300B annual opportunity for us. The regulatory friction is manageable."
