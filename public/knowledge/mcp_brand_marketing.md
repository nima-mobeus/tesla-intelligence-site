# SECTION: COMPETITIVE_BRAND_COMPARE
section_id: brand_competitive

Brand perception comparison (Q1 2030, global survey n=48,000):

| Attribute | Tesla | BYD | Toyota | Mercedes | BMW | Apple |
|----------|-------|-----|--------|---------|-----|-------|
| Innovation | 94% | 72% | 68% | 74% | 76% | 96% |
| Trust | 68% | 58% | 82% | 78% | 76% | 84% |
| Luxury | 72% | 32% | 42% | 92% | 88% | 90% |
| Value | 64% | 84% | 78% | 38% | 42% | 48% |
| Safety | 78% | 62% | 86% | 80% | 78% | N/A |
| Sustainability | 88% | 68% | 76% | 72% | 70% | 74% |
| Excitement | 92% | 48% | 34% | 62% | 68% | 82% |

Key insights:
- Tesla leads on **Innovation, Sustainability, Excitement** — the three attributes most correlated with purchase intent in 18-34 demographic
- Trust gap vs Toyota (68% vs 82%) — driven by CEO political commentary and FSD perception. Robotaxi safety record (42× safer) not yet fully internalized by public
- Luxury perception (72%) lags Mercedes (92%) — not necessarily a negative; Tesla is positioned as "tech premium" not "traditional luxury"
- BYD dominates on Value (84%) — key competitive dimension in price-sensitive markets
